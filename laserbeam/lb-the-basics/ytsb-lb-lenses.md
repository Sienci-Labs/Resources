In order to have a useful laser system, the light from the laser diode needs "modification." This is where laser lenses come in; we need lenses to direct and focus the light for the different applications we may have. You can get a wide variety of results using the same laser but different lenses, so that you can go from cutting ¼" softwoods or opaque acrylic to engraving a very detailed crest on genuine leather.

<strong>Currently, there are 4 lens types that we offer:</strong>

<h3>3-Element lens</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Best for engraving </li>
  <li>Smallest beam size → perfect for detailed engraving</li>
  <li>Lowest power output → may not cut as well as other lenses</li>
  <li>Medium focal length </li>
</ul>

<h3>G2 lens</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Best for cutting </li>
  <li>Largest beam size → perfect for bulk material cutting</li>
  <li>Highest power output → can cut through material with less passes</li>
  <li>Smallest focal length → can cut thicker material due to low Z-axis travel constraint</li>
</ul>
<h4><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2324" src="https://resources.sienci.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/g2_B-850x566.jpg" alt="" width="850" height="566" /></h4>
<h3>G7 lens (Balanced Multi-Purpose Perfection)</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Good for basic cutting and engraving</li>
  <li>Medium beam size</li>
  <li>Good power output</li>
  <li>Largest focal length → can cut/engrave thicker material than G8 lens</li>
</ul>
