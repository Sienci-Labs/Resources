Let's perform a quick test to make sure the LaserBeam fires up.

Turn on your LaserBeam driver. Refer to the section <a href="https://resources.sienci.com/view/lb-turning-driver-on/">Turning Driver On</a> to make sure you successfully power it on.

Navigate to the Spindle/Laser tab at the bottom of the screen and select <b>"SLB_LASER"</b> in the dropdown list. After this, flip the toggle at the top to <b>"Laser Mode."</b>

<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10422 aligncenter" src="https://resources.sienci.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/slb_up_p38_SLBSpindle.jpg" alt="" width="623" height="286" />

Ensure your power is set to<b> 1%</b> and your "<b>Test Duration"</b> to <b>1 second</b>. Now when you hit the "Laser Test" button your diode should momentarily emit a faint beam. This means that it is controlling your laser successfully!

<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10423 aligncenter" src="https://resources.sienci.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/slb_up_p39_LaserTest.jpg" alt="" width="594" height="350" />

<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-10814 aligncenter" src="https://resources.sienci.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/laser-test.png" alt="" width="960" height="540" />
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>Successful laser test!</i></p>
If the laser testing didn't go as expected, check your wiring and DIP switches, and review the instructions for setting up and turning on your driver.
