<h2>Mounting ü™õ</h2>
There are a few different ways in which you can go about mounting your Vortex Riser onto your LongMill router mount. These mounting orientations vary based on your LongMill model.
<ul>
  <li>Models <b>MK1V1 - V4 (Mark 1 version 1 to 4)</b> router mounts possess holes for mounting on their side¬†</li>
  <li>Models <b>MK1V4B - MK2 (Mark 1 Version 4B to Mark 2)</b>¬† possess mounting holes on their front face:</li>
</ul>
[gallery columns="2" link="none" size="medium" ids="6222,6221"]

Let's first go through the two ways to mount your Vortex Riser if you have a model <strong>MK1V4B-V2</strong>.
<h3 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Orientation 1</strong></h3>
<strong>1.</strong> Firstly, use an appropriate allen key to remove the circled M5 bolts from your router mount:

[gallery columns="2" size="medium" link="none" ids="6424,6437"]

&nbsp;

<strong>2.</strong> Line up the circled holes with the location of the removed bolts:

[gallery columns="2" size="large" link="none" ids="6430,6431"]

&nbsp;

<strong>3.¬†</strong>Re-insert the M5 bolts previously removed in step 1 and tighten them with your allen key:

&nbsp;
<h3 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Orientation 2</strong></h3>
<strong>1.</strong> Like with orientation one, we firstly want use an appropriate allen key to remove the circled M5 bolts from your router mount.

[gallery columns="2" link="none" size="medium" ids="6464,6439"]

<strong>3.</strong> Align the mounting holes and re-insert the previously removed M5 bolts:
