## Spindle Mounting

1. From the **Accessories box** in the main ATC box, take out the fasteners.
2. Using the **top foam as a cushion**, remove the spindle from the bottom foam. Place the spindle on the top foam with the **threaded holes facing up**.

   > **Important:** Never stand the spindle on its nose (the end where tools are inserted). This can damage the bearings.
3. Take out the **ATC mounting plate** and place it on the spindle.
4. Use this chart provided (based on your setup) to determine where you want to mount the spindle and plate.

![](/_images/_atc/_atc_assembly/atc_assembly_mounting_chart.jpg){.aligncenter .size-medium}

5. Place **four (4) M6-14 mm screws** into the holes and fasten them using the provided Allen key.
6. Place **two (2) M6 shoulder screws** onto the ATC mounting plate, then align them with the holes on the **X gantry** of the AltMill. Secure using the Allen key.
7. Use **two (2) M6-25 mm screws** on the bottom holes of the plate to fully secure the assembly onto the X gantry.

> **Note for 4×8 machines:** Remove the cover of the backpack on the ATC spindle to access more mounting holes on the X gantry. Undo the three screws on the cover, then use **two (2) M6-25 mm screws** to secure the ATC mounting plate to the gantry.

## Drag Chain Routing

1. Grab the **drag chain** from the main ATC box. Undo all clips using a flathead screwdriver.
2. Remove the **male drag chain end link** (the one with studs on the sides, not holes).
3. Place the male link onto the **Z-axis drag chain pickup**, then fasten using **two (2) M4-8 mm screws**.
4. Align the assembled Z-axis drag chain pickup onto the spindle at the threaded holes, then use **four (4) M4-8 mm screws** to securely attach it.
5. From the other side of the drag chain, remove the **female end link** and place it on the **X-axis drag chain pickup**. Secure it with **two bolts and nuts**.
6. Make sure the clips on your existing AltMill drag chain are undone and that the **drag chain mount at the back of the XZ axis is removed** before proceeding.
7. From the existing drag chain, remove the male drag chain end link and attach it to the X-axis drag chain pickup with **M4-8 mm screws**.
8. Attach the assembled X-axis drag chain to the back of the Z-axis assembly using the screws from the previously removed drag chain mount.

## Cable and Tubing Routing

1. Grab the following from the main box:

   * ATC spindle cable (blue)
   * Signal cable
   * Air line tubing

2. On the **Z-axis**, rest one end of the air line tubing and the ends of the spindle and signal cables (with aviation plugs) over the gantry. Ensure they can reach the spindle.

3. Fit the cables and tubing in the space between the Z-axis assembly and the **female end link** on the X-axis drag chain pickup.

   > **Important:** Do not kink the tubing.

4. Attach the Z-axis drag chain from the ATC main box onto the female end link so that the drag chain hangs over the Z-axis, parallel to the cables and tubing.

   > **Warning:** Cables and tubing must run through drag chains in parallel. **Never cross them over each other.**

5. Route the **spindle cable** through the Z, X, then Y-axis drag chains, exiting at the **front of the machine**.

6. Route the **signal cable** through the Z, X, then Y-axis drag chains, exiting at the front. Ensure the end with the **single Molex connector** can reach the SLB-EXT.

7. Route the **air line tubing** through the Z-axis drag chain, leaving a palm-sized loop at the back of the Z assembly for strain relief. Continue routing through the X and Y-axis drag chains, exiting at the **back of the machine** where the air regulator/filter will be placed.

   > **Important:** Avoid bending the tubing while routing to prevent kinks or blockages.
